## Design Context

### Users
Home cooks and ingredient-led food explorers using the app in quick, everyday kitchen moments. Their core job is to turn what they already have at home into simple recipe ideas, pairings, substitutions, and grounded food inspiration without feeling judged, overwhelmed, or pushed into medical-style advice.

### Brand Personality
Calm, premium, and encouraging. The product should feel like a refined kitchen companion: confident enough to guide, warm enough to feel approachable, and clear enough to build trust around food discovery and general nutrition education.

### Aesthetic Direction
Mobile-first editorial utility with soft culinary luxury. Use airy cloud-tinted surfaces, fresh mint-green actions, rounded forms, and spacious typography drawn from the Stitch references. The interface should feel contemporary and polished, not clinical, gamified, or noisy. Favor light-first presentation with room for dark-mode support later.

### Design Principles
1. Make pantry input feel effortless and welcoming, especially from empty states.
2. Keep hierarchy breathable: fewer elements, stronger spacing, clearer calls to action.
3. Let trust stay visible but quiet with grounded educational framing rather than health claims.
4. Use premium softness instead of generic app chrome: rounded shapes, tinted neutrals, and restrained accent color.
5. Build for real kitchen speed on mobile first, then let larger screens expand gracefully.
